What's the Internet Archive?

The Internet Archive is a non-profit that has archived 835 billion web pages, 44 million books and texts, 15 million audio recordings, and much more. Their mission is to “provide Universal Access to All Knowledge”.

Anybody can create an account and upload media to the Internet Archive, and there are also numerous other ways that participants can help out. Some examples include: writing code, fixing broken links on automatically uploaded web pages, and writing descriptions for archived ebooks.

Learn more here.

Why participate?

Participants who contribute to the Internet Archive benefit from positive learning outcomes. Research shows that open source participation in online information repositories can result in mastery of the contributed material, digital competencies, collaborative skill building, and transferable knowledge construction.

Additionally, participants contribute to the Internet Archive's mission of universal access to all knowledge. This archive of over 145+ Petabytes of data promotes the democratization of information by making a large variety of resources available online at no cost to users.

Why this website?

This website seeks to consolidate all opportunities for participation at the Internet Archive into one discoverable place with the goal of increasing participation at the Internet Archive. This website is the result of research which shows that providing organized, actionable, and discoverable goals encourages participation in open source projects.
